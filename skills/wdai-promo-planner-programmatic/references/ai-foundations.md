## Advanced Tier — Separate CTA Required

Advanced is opt-in. Members must take a distinct action to register (separate Luma link). This means:

1. Include a dedicated Advanced CTA in Phase 3 (2 weeks out) — it can be part of the general enrollment reminder but must explicitly call out Advanced as a separate step
2. Post the Advanced CTA to `#aifoundations2-intermediate` (where Intermediate members are already enrolled) so they know to opt in
3. Copy for the Advanced CTA should be specific: "If you want to go deeper in Advanced, that's a separate registration — here's the link."

Do not assume members will self-discover the Advanced option. It requires its own prompt.

---

## Copy Tone for AI Foundations

- **Mission-rooted**: This is about building real AI skills, not just attending a webinar. Write as if it matters — because it does.
- **Specific outcomes**: "You'll build X" beats "You'll learn about AI." Reference real tools, real workflows, things members actually came away with.
- **Community proof**: WDAI members are building things. Show that. "Last cohort, members built [X], [Y], [Z]" is more powerful than any feature list.
- **"Bring a friend" framing**: The best promotion is a personal invite. Encourage members to forward to one specific person who'd benefit.
- **Avoid hype**: "Revolutionary" and "transformative" land flat with this audience. Concrete and honest wins.

## Enrollment Cadence Note

Since enrollment closes on the start date (not before), the promotion arc should peak in the final week — that's the highest-leverage moment. Don't front-load all the urgency into the launch announcement. Save the strongest social proof and clearest CTAs for Phase 3 (2 weeks out) and Phase 4 (1 week out).
