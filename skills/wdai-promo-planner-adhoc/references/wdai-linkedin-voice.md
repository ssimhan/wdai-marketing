# WDAI LinkedIn Voice Guide

Distilled from the WDAI_Event_Posts_Voice_Guide.docx (compiled March 2026). Use this when drafting any WDAI LinkedIn post — whether from the org account or a community member.

## Core Voice Traits

| Trait | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Community-First** | "We" and "our community" appear frequently. The reader is always invited in, never talked at. |
| **Mission-Rooted** | Every event connects back to: women already have what it takes — they need space, tools, and each other. Events are proof of that mission. |
| **Warmly Bold** | Confident and aspirational without being corporate. Strong declarative statements ("They build the table") alongside genuine warmth. |
| **Story-Driven** | Anchor on a specific person, moment, or room — not abstract benefits. Describe what actually happened, who showed up, what shifted. |
| **Permission-Giving** | Lower the barrier actively: "no prior coding experience required", "everyone is at a different stage." The subtext: you belong here. |
| **Understated CTA** | Soft and inviting, not pushy. "Come find out." "Come join us." Focus on belonging, not conversion. |
| **Hashtag Light** | 3–5 hashtags. Mix of broad (#WomenInAI) and specific (#KoraBench). Never performative. |

## Recurring Sentence Patterns

These constructions appear repeatedly and define the WDAI event post voice:

**The contrast setup:**
> "This isn't just a [thing]. It's [deeper meaning]."
> Example: "This isn't just a technical tutorial. It's a space where women can learn new tools together, ask questions without judgment..."

**The earned truth:**
> "That's what happens when women are given the tools, the community, and the permission to experiment."

**The pull quote:**
Member voice anchors emotional credibility. Always a direct quote that captures a feeling, not a feature.

**The soft close:**
> "If that question excites you too — come find out."
> "The most important step is simply to begin."

**The room description** (for event recaps):
> "The room felt open and engaged." "Something shifted in the room."

## For Event Invitations — What to Replicate

- Open with the WHY, not the what
- Use the contrast structure: "This isn't just [X]. It's [deeper thing]."
- State logistics simply and plainly (day, time, what you'll walk away with)
- Lower the barrier: "no prior experience required", "wherever you are in your journey"
- End with a soft, human CTA — never salesy

## For Event Recaps — What to Replicate

- Describe the room: who showed up, what the energy was
- Anchor on one person's story or one specific moment
- Use a direct member quote to carry the emotional weight
- Connect back to mission — what does this moment prove about what WDAI believes?
- Close with an invitation: the community is still open, still growing

## Things to Avoid

- Generic hype language ("Don't miss this!", "Amazing opportunity!")
- Bullet-point event agendas as primary content
- Corporate-sounding language or passive voice
- More than 5 hashtags
- Leading with event logistics (date, time, speaker) before establishing the why
