# Women Defining AI — Brand Guidelines

## Mission

Women Defining AI Inc. is dedicated to educating the public on artificial intelligence through curated learning programs, educational content, research, and community events. We advance this educational mission with a specific focus on closing the gender-tech gap and empowering women and underrepresented groups to lead in the AI era.

---

## Vision

### The Problem
People in underrepresented groups are often disenfranchised and devalued during moments of economic and societal transition. The same risk is present in widespread workplace AI adoption, particularly for women:
- Only 1/3 of women are using AI in their personal & professional lives (compared to more than half of men)
- Women still make up less than 30% of STEM-related jobs

**It doesn't have to be this way.**

### The Solution
To lead thinking around generative AI, you have to know how it works and deeply understand what is possible, what isn't, and where the risks and possibilities are. That's how we get to a future where women are truly **DEFINING AI**.

If we close the gender-tech gap for women, we empower more women to be at the decision-making table—founding companies, investing, buying technology. When we create economic opportunities for women around ownership, we close the gender-financial gap and level the playing field.

## Audience

**Primary:** Women and nonbinary professionals aged 25–50 across industries (tech, finance, marketing, operations, education, nonprofit) who are curious about AI but aren't building it full-time. They have domain expertise in their fields and want to add AI literacy without becoming engineers.

**Secondary:** Leaders and founders looking to empower their teams with hands-on AI skills and practical understanding.

**Tertiary:** Career-changers and founders exploring AI as a tool or business opportunity.

## Tone Principles

1. **Peer-to-peer, not top-down.** We've been where you are. We're figuring this out together, not lecturing from on high.

2. **Conviction without gatekeeping.** We know what we believe and say it clearly. But we welcome questions and different perspectives.

3. **Practical over theoretical.** Show, don't tell. Real examples beat framework explanations. What actually works matters.

4. **Warm and direct.** No corporate softening. We're genuinely excited about this work and not afraid to say so.

5. **Build in public.** Share wins and failures openly. Celebrate learners at all levels. Make space for the messy middle.

6. **Specificity is trust.** Vague language erodes credibility. Cite data, name names, use concrete examples. The granularity is the insight.

7. **Admit what we don't know.** We're not experts in everything. Intellectual humility is strength.

## Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

- Generic openers: "In today's rapidly changing world..."
- Contrast framing: "It's not X, it's Y"
- Corporate abstractions: unlock value, drive innovation, leverage synergies, enablement
- Symmetrical rhetoric: "The challenge isn't the technology — it's the mindset"
- Overused metaphors: rockets, moonshots, hockey sticks
- Tidy conclusions: "The future belongs to those who embrace change"

## Content Guidelines by Format

### Email & Announcements
- Lead with the news, not the setup
- Warm and peer-to-peer
- Specific details about what, when, why
- Clear CTA

### Social (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Lead with observation or insight
- Show the people, not just the concept
- Personal experience as anchor; broader implication as payload
- Authentic celebration of members and wins

### Website Copy
- Problem-first framing (lead with what matters to the reader)
- Concrete examples over abstract benefit statements
- Members and leaders featured by name and story
- Clear, scannable structure with active language

### Long-Form (Essays, Guides)
- Ideas build on each other; no restating
- Mix intellectual and personal perspectives
- Specific details; trust the reader to understand nuance
- Ending inward (reflection, question, tension) not outward (prescriptive conclusion)
